About My Thesis

Welcome to the "About" page of my thesis project on finding the shortest path using Dijkstra's algorithm. This thesis explores the theoretical underpinnings and practical applications of Dijkstra's algorithm in solving the shortest path problem in graphs.

Dijkstra's algorithm, named after Dutch computer scientist Edsger W. Dijkstra, is a popular algorithm used in network routing protocols and geographic mapping services to determine the shortest path between nodes in a graph. The algorithm works by iteratively selecting the node with the smallest tentative distance, updating the distances of its neighboring nodes, and repeating the process until the shortest path to the destination node is found.

In this thesis, we delve into the details of how Dijkstra's algorithm functions, its computational complexity, and its applications in real-world scenarios. We also discuss various optimizations and modifications to the algorithm that can enhance its efficiency and applicability to larger and more complex graphs.
